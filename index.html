POSITION

AI is not a strategy. It is a lever — and like any lever, it breaks the system if you pull it without knowing where the load is.

Most "AI transformations" fail not because the technology is wrong, but because the organisation hasn't done the unglamorous work: documented workflows, identified decision boundaries, mapped data flows, agreed what "good" looks like. We start there.

Average pilot failure rate (industry) ~70%
Typical time-to-value with our process 8–12 weeks
Average cost variance vs. plan < 10%
Engagements that reach production 9 of 10
01 / CURRENT-STATE ASSESSMENT

Before any code, we map the actual state of the operation.

A two-week diagnostic. We sit with the people who actually run the process, trace data, document decisions, and produce a clear, evidence-based picture of what is automatable — and what is not.

A · 01

Process archaeology

We reconstruct the real flow — not the documented one. Shadow the operators, capture screen recordings, pull system logs. Most "broken" automations fail because they automate a process nobody actually follows.

A · 02

Data lineage audit

Where does the data come from, who touches it, what is the quality? We map it on one page, identify the bottlenecks and the integrity risks. AI is only as good as the substrate it reads from.

A · 03

Decision boundaries

Which decisions can be safely delegated to a model, which need a human in the loop, which should never be automated? We define these explicitly — with the people who will live with the consequences.

A · 04

Risk & cost surface

A scorecard of what could go wrong, the blast radius, and the cost — both in build and in run. We deliver this in a single document, ranked, with clear go / no-go thresholds.

02 / WHAT WE AUTOMATE

We work where the leverage is highest — and the blast radius is manageable.

Six areas where AI automation has the strongest track record of producing measurable value without destabilising the operation. We pick one or two — not all six.

C · 01

Document & data extraction

Reading unstructured inputs — contracts, invoices, applications, emails — and turning them into structured records a system can act on.

  • Invoices & receipts → ERP
  • CVs → ATS structured fields
  • Customer emails → ticket + category
  • Contracts → clause & obligation library
C · 02

Customer support augmentation

Drafting responses, summarising long threads, escalating the right cases at the right moment. The human stays in control of the final message.

  • Long-thread summarisation
  • First-draft replies in brand voice
  • Sentiment & churn signal detection
  • Knowledge base gap discovery
C · 03

Reporting & analysis

Replacing the weekly "pull numbers together" ritual with a continuously updated view — and an analyst who actually explains what changed and why.

  • Weekly business review automation
  • Anomaly detection with explanation
  • Cohort & funnel analysis on demand
  • Board-deck narrative drafting
C · 04

Sales & revenue ops

Lead enrichment, outreach personalisation at scale, and the unglamorous work of cleaning CRM data so the team can actually trust it.

  • Lead scoring with rationale
  • Personalised outreach drafts
  • CRM hygiene & dedup
  • Proposal & RFP first-drafts
C · 05

Compliance & quality

Reviewing 100% of what humans would review 5% of. Flagging the cases that need a human, not replacing the human.

  • AML / KYC first-pass screening
  • Customer communication review
  • Policy & procedure compliance
  • Internal audit evidence gathering
C · 06

Internal knowledge ops

Making 10 years of internal documents actually findable, summarisable, and useful — without anyone having to "ask IT".

  • Internal search & Q&A
  • Onboarding & training copilots
  • Meeting & decision summarisation
  • Cross-team knowledge graphs
